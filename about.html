About Ama

Ama is my 20 years old sister and a great inspiration to me personally.
inspires me most about Ama is her ability to genuinely empathize with others and her relentless nature of always wanting to help other people however she can.
Ama is currently in her junior year of college at Sam Houston University majoring in psychology and a minor in biology.
Ama was born in Nigeria and migrated with her family to the United States when she was two years old.
Ama has lived in four different states: Iowa, Chicago Illinois, Bronx New York, and Houston Texas.
Between 8th grade and 10th grade, Ama went to high school in Nigeria, which she considers to be a period of cultural exposure and adventure during that point of her life.
Ama has several hobbies, some of her favorites are, crocheting, Interior designing, taking trips with her friends. Ama is very social and outgoing and enjoys helping other people however she can.
Her dream is to become a clinical psychologist, where she feels she will be able to help others navigate through difficulties in life.

An Interview with Ama

What motivates you?

I want to live a life free of unnecessary personal stress and pressure.
I want to spend most of my time helping others and having personal problems will limit my ability to do so.
Keeping this in mind motivates me to do what I have to do today to ensure
I get to live the life I want in the future. Seeing my mom work so hard when I was younger also motivates me.

What are your hobbies?

I enjoy crocheting, I enjoy spending time with my friends, I also enjoy interior designing.

Where do you see yourself in five years?

In 5 years I would’ve been admitted into my desired school for my physician assistant studies.

What is your greatest achievement so far in your life?

So far would be successfully making it to my junior year in college. Most people may think it’s easy to do,
I see it as a great accomplishment so far.

How would you describe your character in four words?

Empathetic, loving, passionate, and determined

As a child, what made you happy the most?

well, I gain most of my happiness from seeing other people happy and I think
that has always been the case even as a child.

What is your greatest fear?

Failure. that’s my greatest fear. I don’t mean the type of failure you
have to learn from but being deemed as a failure in life.

Where have you lived before?

Iowa, New York, Chicago, and Houston.

What is an interesting fact about you?

This might be a cliché, but I have an intense fear of heights.

How would you describe your cultural identity?

Well, growing up in a very culture rich family has certainly been a plus in my life.
I truly appreciate my Nigerian culture, but I sometimes find myself struggling To fit into
my culture of origin, since I have been away from it for so long.
